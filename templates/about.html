About this project

A Sentiment Model For
GEN Z Gamer Slang

A sentiment model that actually (somewhat) understands gaming slang, because "this update is mid" and "this game is goated" mean very different things, and most tools can't tell, that isn't ai.

The problem

Generic sentiment tools are trained on movie reviews, product feedback, and formal writing. They don't know that "cooked" can mean ruined or excellent depending on context, that "mid" means mediocre, or that "W" and "L" carry real sentiment. Gaming communities have their own language thus this model was built to speak it.

How it works

The model started with thousands of REAL Steam reviews, each already labeled positive or negative by the people who wrote them. On top of that, hundreds of custom examples were added, covering slang, negation ("I do not hate this"), sarcasm, and comparisons ("way better than the last update") to teach it the patterns generic models miss. A pretrained language model was then fine-tuned on all of it, combining real language understanding with gaming-specific vocabulary.

Known limitations

No neutral category, every input is read as positive or negative, even purely factual statements.
Idioms like "not bad" or "could be worse" can occasionally be misread.
Tuned for English gaming slang. Singlish and other regional phrases aren't yet covered.
